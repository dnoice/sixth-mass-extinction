### 2.3 Valuation Methodologies

#### Primary Methods

| Method | Application | Strengths | Limitations |
|--------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Market Price** | Traded goods (timber, fish) | Observable, objective | Ignores externalities |
| **Replacement Cost** | Water purification, flood control | Practical baseline | May under/overestimate |
| **Avoided Cost** | Disaster prevention | Clear counterfactual | Requires damage estimates |
| **Production Function** | Agricultural inputs | Links to GDP | Complex modeling |
| **Travel Cost** | Recreation/tourism | Revealed preference | Limited to recreation |
| **Hedonic Pricing** | Property values | Market-based | Isolating nature effect difficult |
| **Contingent Valuation** | Existence/bequest value | Captures non-use | Hypothetical bias |
| **Choice Experiments** | Multiple attributes | Trade-off analysis | Complex design |
| **Benefit Transfer** | Rapid assessment | Cost-effective | Context sensitivity |

#### Challenges in Valuation

1. **Non-linearity:** Ecosystem collapse creates discontinuous value loss
1. **Irreversibility:** Extinction cannot be assigned finite cost
1. **Uncertainty:** Future service provision unknown
1. **Substitutability:** Many services have no substitutes
1. **Distribution:** Benefits and costs fall on different groups
1. **Discounting:** How to value future generations' interests
