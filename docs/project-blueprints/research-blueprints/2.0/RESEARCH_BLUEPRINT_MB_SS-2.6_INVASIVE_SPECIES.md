## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Article Focus

This research blueprint guides the creation of a comprehensive article examining **"How do invasive species and disease drive biodiversity collapse?"** by documenting biological invasions as systematic ecosystem dismantling from the "most destructive pathogen ever described by science" causing 90 amphibian extinctions to feral cats threatening 430 species to $423 billion in annual economic costs, revealing trade-driven pathogen spread and climate-amplified invasion as accelerating extinction drivers.

### Target Output

- **Stand-alone article:** 8,000-10,000 words
- **Academic rigor:** Suitable for peer review or environmental journalism
- **Accessibility:** Comprehensible to educated general audience
- **Evidence density:** Every invasion pathway documented and sourced

### Core Thesis

Invasive species and disease have become primary extinction drivers—with 3,500 invasive species causing 60% of documented extinctions, the chytrid fungus alone responsible for 90 amphibian species lost and 124 declining >90%, feral cats threatening 430 species across islands, ballast water transporting 7,000 species daily, and economic costs of $423 billion annually quadrupling every decade—amplified by global trade vectors, climate change expanding suitable habitats, and policy failures (80% countries have targets, only 17% have laws).
