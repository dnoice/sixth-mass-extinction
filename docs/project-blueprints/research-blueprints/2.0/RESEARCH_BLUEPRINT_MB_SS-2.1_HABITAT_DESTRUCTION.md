## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Article Focus

This research blueprint guides the creation of a comprehensive article examining **"What drives habitat destruction and who is responsible?"** by documenting the conversion of ecosystems to commodities (beef, soy, palm oil, timber), tracing corporate supply chains, and quantifying the single largest extinction driver: land use change.

### Target Output

- **Stand-alone article:** 8,000-10,000 words
- **Corporate attribution:** Names, maps, supply chains for JBS, Cargill, Wilmar, ADM
- **Commodity emphasis:** Beef, soy, palm oil, timber traced to consumer products
- **Scale integration:** Global patterns to specific corporate concessions

### Core Thesis

Habitat destruction—primarily agricultural expansion for cattle (70-80% of Amazon clearing), soy, and palm oil—is the dominant extinction driver, converting 10 million hectares of forest annually while draining 35% of wetlands since 1970. Specific corporations (JBS, Cargill, Wilmar, ADM) operate identifiable supply chains connecting consumer products to ecosystem collapse, making this a crisis of corporate accountability as much as agricultural policy.

## RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

### Primary Questions to Answer

1. **What is the scale and pace of global habitat destruction across ecosystem types?**
1. **Which commodities drive deforestation and habitat conversion, and where?**
1. **Which corporations control the supply chains converting ecosystems to products?**
1. **How does habitat loss translate to species extinction at different scales?**
1. **What makes certain ecosystems (wetlands, tropical forests) most vulnerable?**

### Secondary Questions

1. How do agricultural expansion patterns differ by region (Amazon vs. Congo vs. SE Asia)?
1. What role do policy interventions (moratoria, protected areas) play in slowing destruction?
1. How effective is indigenous land management at preventing deforestation?
1. What are the ecological consequences of habitat fragmentation vs. outright loss?
1. How close are major ecosystems to irreversible tipping points driven by clearing?

### 8. Equating All Deforestation Drivers

**Pitfall:** Treating smallholder and industrial agriculture equally
**Problem:** Misses scale differences, corporate responsibility
**Solution:** Quantify: Industrial commodity agriculture is largest driver (cattle 70-80% in Amazon)

### 9. Ignoring Indigenous Effectiveness

**Pitfall:** Not contrasting deforestation rates on indigenous vs. other lands
**Problem:** Misses proven solution (land rights)
**Solution:** Show comparative data (Walker et al., Fa et al.), case studies, honor defenders

### 10. Map Overload Without Narrative

**Pitfall:** Many maps but unclear story
**Problem:** Reader overwhelmed, misses key points
**Solution:** Each map serves specific argument, clear captions, text integration
