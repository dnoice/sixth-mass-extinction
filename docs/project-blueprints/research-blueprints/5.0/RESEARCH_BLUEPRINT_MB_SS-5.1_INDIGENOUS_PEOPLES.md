## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Article Focus

This research blueprint guides creation of a comprehensive article examining **Indigenous peoples as frontline resistors to ecological collapse** - documenting the systematic violence against environmental defenders, the superior conservation outcomes of Indigenous land stewardship, the structural impunity protecting perpetrators, and the corporate/state complicity driving encroachment on Indigenous territories.

### Target Output

- **Stand-alone article:** 8,000-10,000 words
- **Sovereignty-centered analysis:** Rights first, conservation second
- **Violence documentation:** 2,253 killed (2012-2024); 146 in 2024; 33% Indigenous
- **Conservation effectiveness:** 66-83% lower deforestation; 340M tonnes CO2 sequestered
- **Named accountability:** Corporations, governments, perpetrators identified

### Core Thesis

Indigenous peoples are the world's most effective environmental stewards - managing ~25% of Earth's land surface with 66-83% lower deforestation rates than state-managed areas, storing 340 million tonnes of CO2 annually in Amazon territories alone. Yet they face systematic violence: 2,253 environmental defenders killed since 2012, with Indigenous peoples comprising 33% of victims despite being only 6% of global population. This violence is enabled by structural impunity (94.8% of killings in Colombia go unprosecuted), corporate complicity (mining, cattle, soy, lithium industries), and state facilitation. Indigenous peoples are not passive victims but active resistors whose sovereignty must be centered in any genuine response to ecological collapse.

### Critical Framing Note

**The widely-cited claim that "80% of biodiversity is on Indigenous lands" is UNSUPPORTED and must NOT be used.** A 2024 Nature Commentary debunked this statistic, finding no empirical basis. Instead, use verified data: Indigenous territories contain 37% of remaining natural lands, 33% of intact forest landscapes, and demonstrate 66-83% lower deforestation rates.
