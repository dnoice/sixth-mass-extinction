## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Article Focus

This research blueprint guides creation of a comprehensive article examining why **current conservation approaches are insufficient** to halt the sixth mass extinction. The focus is not on blaming conservationists - many are doing heroic work under impossible conditions - but on documenting the structural, financial, and political constraints that prevent conservation from succeeding at the scale required.

### Target Output

- **Stand-alone article:** 8,000-10,000 words
- **Protected area assessment:** Coverage, effectiveness, "paper parks"
- **Funding gap analysis:** $942 billion shortfall documented
- **Climate adaptation challenge:** Static reserves vs. dynamic species
- **Ex-situ limitations:** Zoos, seed banks, captive breeding reality check
- **Indigenous comparison:** 2.5x more effective conservation
- **Systemic critique:** Why conservation fails within current system

### Core Thesis

Current conservation efforts are **necessary but grossly insufficient** to halt the sixth mass extinction. Protected areas cover only **17.6% of land and 8.4% of ocean** - far short of the 30% target for 2030. Even existing protected areas are failing: **73% experienced habitat alteration between 2003-2019**, with **1.14 million km² of habitat** lost within supposedly protected boundaries. These are not "protected" areas - they are **paper parks**. Marine protection is even worse: only **2.6% of the global ocean** is under high or full protection, despite MPAs covering 8.4% on paper. The **$942 billion annual funding gap** means protected areas are chronically underfunded, with less than 5% having management effectiveness assessed. Static protected areas **cannot track climate-driven range shifts** - species are moving poleward at 17 km per decade while reserve boundaries remain fixed. Ex-situ conservation (zoos, seed banks) can save only a **tiny fraction of species**: zoos can sustain perhaps **900 species** at viable population sizes; seed banks have collected **14% of plant species**. The one bright spot: **Indigenous-managed lands are 2.5x more effective** at preventing habitat loss than government-managed areas. Yet Indigenous peoples continue to be excluded from conservation decision-making. Conservation cannot succeed while the economic system driving destruction remains unchanged. We are treating the symptoms while feeding the disease.

### Critical Framing Note

**This article does not argue against conservation.** Protected areas, when properly funded and managed, are essential. Conservation biologists and rangers are often heroes working against impossible odds. But we must be honest: **conservation within the current economic and political system cannot halt mass extinction.** The drivers of extinction - habitat destruction, climate change, overexploitation - are systemic. Conservation is a **rear-guard action** that cannot win while the main assault continues. This is not an argument to abandon conservation, but to recognize that **conservation alone is not enough.** We need both protection AND transformation.
