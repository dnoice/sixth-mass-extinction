## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Article Focus

This research blueprint guides creation of a comprehensive article examining **"How governments actively enable mass extinction through subsidies, weak enforcement, regulatory capture, and international coordination failure"** by documenting the $7.7 trillion subsidy contradiction (destruction funded 11x more than protection), revolving door between industry and regulators, and near-total impunity for environmental crimes.

### Target Output

- **Stand-alone article:** 7,000-9,000 words
- **Subsidy quantification:** $7T fossil + $842B ag + $35B fishing vs $700B biodiversity gap
- **Enforcement documentation:** 27% paper parks, <10% prosecution, 146 defenders killed in 2024
- **Capture mechanisms:** Revolving door personnel tracking, lobbying expenditure analysis

### Core Thesis

Governments don't fail to prevent extinction through incompetence—they actively enable it through design. $7.7 trillion in annual subsidies for fossil fuels, agriculture, and fishing dwarf the $700 billion biodiversity funding gap (11:1 ratio favoring destruction). Regulatory capture via revolving door (39% of US nominees have fossil fuel ties) and lobbying dominance ($150M+ fossil fuels vs $16M environmental groups) ensures industry writes its own regulations. Enforcement is deliberately weak: 27% of protected areas are "paper parks," fines average <5% of corporate profits, and 2,253 environmental defenders have been killed (2012-2024) with <10% prosecution rate. International frameworks like Paris Agreement and ISA lack any enforcement mechanism. This is not failure—it's the system functioning as designed to protect capital accumulation over planetary survival.
