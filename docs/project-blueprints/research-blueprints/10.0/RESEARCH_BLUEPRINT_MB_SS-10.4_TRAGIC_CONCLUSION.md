## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Article Focus

This research blueprint guides creation of the final synthesis article in the Sixth Mass Extinction Documentation Project - the **honest, unflinching assessment of why the transformation required to halt mass extinction almost certainly will not happen** in time. Not because it's impossible, but because the political, economic, psychological, and institutional obstacles are too deeply entrenched, and historical precedent offers no reassurance. This is not nihilism or despair - it is probability assessment based on evidence. We document the barriers not to excuse them, but to ensure the historical record is clear: **we knew what was happening, we knew what was required, and we chose not to act**.

### Target Output

- **Stand-alone synthesis article:** 8,000-10,000 words
- **Political obstacles:** Corporate capture, $7T subsidies, electoral short-termism, COP failures
- **Economic obstacles:** Growth dependency, sunk costs, stranded assets, vested interests
- **Psychological obstacles:** Denial, distancing, doom fatigue, false hope
- **Institutional obstacles:** Path dependency, inadequate governance, coordination failure
- **Historical precedent:** Civilizations that knew and failed anyway

### Core Thesis

**The obstacles to the required transformation are not accidental - they are structural features of the same systems causing the crisis.** Corporate capture of governance means $7 trillion in fossil fuel subsidies continues while climate finance lags. Electoral cycles of 2-6 years cannot address crises unfolding over decades. The growth imperative makes degrowth politically impossible. Psychological defenses - denial, distancing, doom fatigue - prevent sustained engagement. None of the 20 Aichi Biodiversity Targets were achieved; global emissions hit record highs in 2024 despite 30 years of climate conferences; 1,600 fossil fuel lobbyists attended COP30. **We have had three decades of warning and three decades of failure.** Historical precedent is consistent: civilizations that degraded their ecological base - knowing it was happening - collapsed anyway. The Maya knew. Easter Islanders knew. We know. Knowing is not enough when structural power blocks action. This article is the Cassandra warning: **the truth must be documented for whatever civilization (if any) comes after, so they understand what happened and why.** This is not despair; it is responsibility to the historical record.

### Critical Framing Note

**This article walks a razor's edge.** Too much despair enables paralysis and surrender. Too much hope enables complacency and delay. The honest middle is: **the probability of achieving required transformation in time is very low, but not zero; and regardless of probability, the effort remains morally required.** We document the obstacles not to justify inaction but to ensure future generations (or future civilizations) understand why we failed. The Cassandra curse was not that she was wrong - it was that she was right and no one listened. This article listens to the evidence and reports what it says.
