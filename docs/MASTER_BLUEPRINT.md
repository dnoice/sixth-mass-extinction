## 1. ESTABLISHING THE CRISIS - SCOPE AND SCALE

### 1.1 Baseline vs. Current Extinction Rates

**Background Rate:**
- Natural "background" extinction: 0.1-2 extinctions per million species-years (E/MSY)
- Typical baseline: 1-5 species per year globally

**Current Rate:**
- Modern vertebrate extinctions: **100-1000x background rate**
- IPBES: "Extinction rates tens to hundreds of times higher than background and rising"

**Evidence Base:**
- **[S2]** Ceballos, Ehrlich & Dirzo (2015): Modern extinction rates vastly above background; E/MSY methodology
- **[S3]** Ceballos et al. (2017): "Biological annihilation" - severe population declines beyond extinction counts
- **[S1]** IPBES (2019): Five direct drivers; extinction risk far above background

**Critical Note:** The often-cited "150-200 species per day" lacks robust empirical support. Authoritative assessments emphasize relative rate multipliers and threat proportions instead.

## APPENDIX: KEY TERMS & DEFINITIONS

**Anthropocene:** Geological epoch defined by human impact as dominant influence on Earth systems.

**Background Extinction Rate:** Natural rate without human influence (0.1-2 E/MSY, typically 1-5 species/year globally).

**Biotic Homogenization:** Process where specialist species are replaced by generalist "everywhere species," reducing functional diversity.

**Cascading Extinction / Coextinction:** When loss of one species causes dependent species (parasites, mutualists, specialists) to also go extinct.

**E/MSY:** Extinctions per Million Species-Years, standard unit for measuring extinction rates.

**Extinction Debt:** Species committed to extinction due to habitat loss or other pressures but not yet extinct; time-lagged extinction.

**Extinction Vortex:** Self-reinforcing cycle where small populations face genetic bottlenecks, reduced fitness, further population decline, and eventual extinction.

**Functionally Extinct:** Population too small to fulfill ecological role even if not technically extinct.

**Living Planet Index (LPI):** Measures average change in monitored vertebrate population sizes; does NOT measure species extinctions.

**Paper Parks:** Protected areas that exist on maps but lack effective enforcement or management.

**Planetary Boundaries:** Nine Earth system thresholds defining safe operating space for humanity (RockstrÃ¶m et al., 2009; updated 2023).

**Shifting Baseline Syndrome:** Each generation accepts degraded environmental state as normal, failing to recognize cumulative decline.

**Solastalgia:** Distress caused by environmental change in one's home environment.

**Tipping Point:** Critical threshold beyond which small additional change produces large, often irreversible system transformation.

**Trophic Cascade:** Ecological changes propagating through food web following addition/removal of top predator or keystone species.
