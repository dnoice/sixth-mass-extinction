| Period | Documented Extinctions | Rate (species/century) |

5. **Cumulative and accelerating:** Impacts compound over time

### 2.2 Continental Megafauna Extinctions

#### Africa: The Anomaly

**Why Africa Retained Megafauna:**

- Longest human-wildlife co-evolution (~300,000 years)
- Animals evolved behavioral responses to humans
- Humans evolved with, not into, African ecosystems
- Still lost some megafauna (giant baboon, long-horned buffalo)

**African Pleistocene Extinctions (~20 species):**

- *Pelorovis* (giant long-horned buffalo)
- *Megantereon* (sabertooth cat)
- *Theropithecus oswaldi* (giant baboon)
- *Elephas recki* (giant elephant)
- African giant horse

#### Australia: First Major Collapse (~85% megafauna lost)

**Timing:** 50,000‚Äì40,000 BP (correlates with human arrival ~65,000 BP)

**Lost Species (~40+ species):**

| Species | Size | Last Record |
|---------|------|-------------|
| *Diprotodon optatum* | 2,700 kg | ~44,000 BP |
| *Procoptodon goliah* | 240 kg | ~46,000 BP |
| *Thylacoleo carnifex* (marsupial lion) | 130 kg | ~46,000 BP |
| *Megalania prisca* (giant monitor) | 600 kg | ~40,000 BP |

| Giant wombats (multiple species) | 200+ kg | ~40,000 BP |
| Giant echidna | 30 kg | ~50,000 BP |

**Evidence for Human Causation:**

- Extinction timing matches human arrival
- Eggshell isotopes show *Genyornis* diet change before extinction
- Fire regime changes correlate with human presence
- Climate was stable during extinction window

#### North America: Catastrophic Loss (~35 genera)

**Timing:** 15,000‚Äì10,000 BP (shortly after human arrival via Beringia)

**Lost Genera (~35):**

| Genus | Species | Last Record |

**Scale of Loss:**

- 35 of 51 large mammal genera (>44 kg) extinct
- 68% of megafauna genera lost
- 80%+ of species over 1,000 kg extinct
- 100% of proboscideans (mammoths, mastodons) extinct

#### South America: Greatest Diversity Loss (~50+ genera)

**Timing:** 13,000‚Äì8,000 BP

**Lost Genera:**

- Giant ground sloths (*Megatherium*, *Eremotherium*, *Mylodon*)
- Glyptodonts (*Glyptodon*, *Doedicurus*)

- *Toxodon* (rhinoceros-like)
- *Macrauchenia* (camelid-like)
- Giant armadillos
- Bear-dog (*Arctotherium*)

**Scale:**

- 52 of 60+ megafauna genera extinct
- ~83% of large mammal genera lost
- Unique evolutionary lineages completely eliminated

#### Eurasia: Gradual Decline (~20 species)

**Timing:** Spread over 40,000 years (less synchronized than other continents)

**Lost Species:**

| Species | Size | Last Record | Location |

- Refugia in remote areas (mammoths on Wrangel Island until ~4,000 BP)

### 2.3 The Overkill Hypothesis vs. Climate Change

#### Evidence for Human Causation (Overkill Hypothesis)

| Evidence | Support Level |
|----------|---------------|
| Timing correlates with human arrival | Strong |
| Africa retained megafauna (long co-evolution) | Strong |
| Islands match pattern (late arrival = late extinction) | Strong |
| Selectivity for large species | Strong |
| Archaeological kill sites | Moderate |
| Extinction survived previous climate cycles | Strong |

#### Evidence for Climate Causation

| Evidence | Support Level |
|----------|---------------|
| Pleistocene-Holocene transition | Moderate (timing doesn't match all regions) |
| Habitat changes | Moderate |
| No extinctions in previous interglacials | Contradicts hypothesis |

#### Current Scientific Consensus

**Synergistic Model:**

- Human hunting was primary driver
- Climate change contributed in some regions
- No climate event explains selectivity for large species
- Pattern is too consistent across different climate zones

**Quote (Paul Martin, 2005):**
> "Without *Homo sapiens*, the late Pleistocene extinctions would not have occurred."

### 3.2 Early Civilizations and Environmental Impact

#### Mesopotamia (5,500‚Äì2,000 BP)

**Environmental Transformations:**

- Irrigation systems diverted 60%+ of Tigris-Euphrates flow
- Salinization destroyed agricultural productivity
- Forests of Lebanon/Syria logged for construction
- Lion populations declined (hunting for kings)

**Species Impacts:**

- Mesopotamian lion: Regional extinction by ~100 CE
- Wild aurochs: Range contracted
- Asian elephant: Disappeared from Middle East
- Onager: Populations severely reduced

#### Ancient Egypt (5,000‚Äì2,000 BP)

**Nile Valley Transformation:**

- Wetlands drained for agriculture
- Papyrus marshes reduced 90%+
- Hunting of crocodiles, hippos, large cats

**Species Impacts:**

- Nile hippos: Extinct by 1800 CE (began declining in antiquity)
- Nile crocodile: Disappeared from Egypt by 1700s
- Lions: Last wild Egyptian lion ~1100 CE
- Cheetah: Extinct in Egypt by ~1850 CE (decline began much earlier)

#### Indus Valley (4,500‚Äì3,500 BP)

**Environmental Impacts:**

- Deforestation for brick kilns and construction
- Agricultural expansion in floodplains
- Possible role in civilization collapse (deforestation ‚Üí erosion)
