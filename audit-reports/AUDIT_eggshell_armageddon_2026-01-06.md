## Detailed Findings

### Issue #1: Yangtze Giant Softshell Turtle Count [ERROR]

**Location:** Line 83

**Current Text:**
> "The Yangtze giant softshell turtle once inhabited rivers across China and Vietnam. One confirmed individual remains—a single animal representing an entire species, waiting for a mate that almost certainly does not exist."

**Problem:**
This statement is **outdated**. As of 2023-2025, there are **2-3 known individuals**, not one:

| Individual | Location | Status |
| ---------- | -------- | ------ |
| Male | Suzhou Zoo, China | Confirmed alive |
| Unknown sex | Xuan Khanh Lake, Hanoi, Vietnam | Confirmed 2020s |
| Unknown | Dong Mo Lake, Vietnam | Believed present |

**Critical Context:**
The **last known female died in April 2023** at Lake Dong Mo, Vietnam. The species is now considered **functionally extinct** due to lack of fertile females, but the "one individual" claim is factually incorrect.

**Source:** [Mongabay - Death of last female](https://news.mongabay.com/2023/05/death-of-last-female-yangtze-softshell-turtle-signals-end-for-god-turtle/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yangtze_giant_softshell_turtle)

**Recommended Correction:**
> "The Yangtze giant softshell turtle once inhabited rivers across China and Vietnam. Only two or three individuals are now known to exist—and with the death of the last confirmed female in 2023, the species is functionally extinct, its remaining males waiting for mates that no longer exist."

### Issue #2: CITES Turtle Trade Figure [ERROR]

**Location:** Line 89

**Current Text:**
> "CITES data records over 1.1 billion turtles legally exported between 1990 and 2020."

**Problem:**
The **1.1 billion figure could not be verified** in CITES databases or peer-reviewed literature. Available data suggests significantly lower numbers:

| Source | Period | Figure |
| ------ | ------ | ------ |
| CITES wild trade analysis | 1990-2010 | ~2 million wild individuals |
| U.S. LEMIS data | 2013-2018 | 18.4 million (all turtles) |
| Louisiana state records | Multi-year | 81 million (single state) |

**Analysis:**
- The 1.1 billion figure would require ~37 million turtles exported annually for 30 years
- This may include captive-bred animals (especially red-eared sliders) rather than wild-caught
- No peer-reviewed source citing this specific figure was found

**Source:** [BioOne - Short Review of International Trade](https://bioone.org/journals/chelonian-conservation-and-biology/volume-15/issue-2/CCB-1216.1/A-Short-Review-of-the-International-Trade-of-Wild-Tortoises/10.2744/CCB-1216.1.full)

**Note:** A separate search found "1.1 million sea turtles" **illegally** poached (not billion, not legal)—this may be the source of confusion.

**Recommended Correction:**
Either provide a source citation for the 1.1 billion figure, or revise to:
> "The global turtle trade has moved hundreds of millions of animals over recent decades, with CITES recording millions of wild-caught individuals and the total—including captive-bred specimens—reaching into the hundreds of millions."

OR if a source exists:
> "CITES data records over 1.1 billion turtles legally exported between 1990 and 2020 [Citation needed]."

### Issue #3: Turtle/Tortoise 61% Threatened [CAUTION]

**Location:** Line 27

**Current Text:**
> "Turtles and tortoises—360 species, 61 percent threatened—carry the heaviest burden"

**Problem:**
The **61% figure may be outdated** or includes a different methodology. More recent assessments show:

| Source | Year | Percentage |
| ------ | ---- | ---------- |
| Turtle Conservation Coalition | 2020 | 51.9% Threatened |
| IUCN/TCC update | 2025 | 54% at risk |
| Article claim | - | 61% |

**Context:**
The 61% figure appears in some sources as "threatened **or already extinct**" rather than currently threatened. The Turtle Conservation Coalition's 2020 analysis found 51.9% threatened using IUCN categories.

**Source:** [Mongabay - 54% at risk](https://news.mongabay.com/2025/08/new-report-warns-54-of-turtles-and-tortoises-are-at-risk-of-extinction/)

**Recommended Correction:**
> "Turtles and tortoises—360 species, with more than half threatened—carry the heaviest burden"

OR if using the higher figure:
> "Turtles and tortoises—360 species, up to 61 percent threatened or extinct—carry the heaviest burden"

---

### Issue #4: Atrazine Usage Ranking [MINOR]

**Location:** Line 63

**Current Text:**
> "Atrazine—the second most widely used herbicide on Earth"

**Problem:**
Atrazine's ranking varies by metric (volume, area treated, country):
- Glyphosate is consistently #1 globally
- Atrazine is often cited as #2 in the **United States**, not necessarily globally
- 2,4-D and other herbicides may rank higher globally

**Source:** [UC Berkeley](https://news.berkeley.edu/2010/03/01/frogs/)

**Recommended Correction:**
> "Atrazine—one of the most widely used herbicides on Earth, and the second most common in the United States"
