## 2. The Heartbeat of the World: The Background Rate
To understand the magnitude of what happened to George, and what is happening to thousands of other lineages, we must first understand the rhythm of a healthy world.

Extinction is not inherently evil. It is a natural biological process, as essential as birth. In a functioning biosphere, species compete, climates drift, and niches shift. Old forms give way to new ones. Paleontologists call this constant, low-level turnover the **Background Extinction Rate**.

Think of it as the planetâ€™s resting heart rate. It is the hum of the machine working normally.

Scientists measure this pulse in a specific metric: **E/MSY** (Extinctions per Million Species-Years).
*   **The Normal Pulse:** For vertebrates, the fossil record tells us the background rate is between **0.1 and 1.0 E/MSY**.
*   **The Translation:** In a world with 10,000 mammal species, you would expect oneâ€”just oneâ€”to disappear every 100 to 1,000 years.

In a healthy world, extinction is boring. It is a rare, localized event that takes centuries to unfold.

For our analysis, we have made a strategic choice. We are not using the standard 1.0 baseline. We are using a **"High" Background Rate of 2.0 E/MSY**. We are deliberately doubling the natural rate in our models. We are giving humanity the benefit of the doubt, stacking the deck *against* the conclusion that we are in a crisis.

If we find a signal even after dampening the noise, that signal is undeniable.
